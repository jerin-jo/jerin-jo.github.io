Situation

Amazon Fresh lacked the ability to identify root causes at scale and caused delays in resolution of issues with significant impact on store ops and customer experience.
Task
  • Implement a comprehensive defect waterfall for failed scans to map the root cause of failure to each scan failure
  • Define key defect types causing failed scans
  • Establish clear defect attribution methodology for each failed scan
  • Create central repository for failed scans data
  • Integrate the data and waterfall methods with existing Defects WBR process
Action
  • Led stakeholder interviews and data analysis to understand root causes of failed scans and identify areas for improvement
  • Defined 5 defects accounting for ~25% of all instore failed scans working closely with the AFS Buyability Defects PM
  • Helped create resolution paths for each of the defect buckets defined through existing F3RS SOPs by working with the AFS Buyability Defects PM
  • Established central repository at \ANT\dept\AmazonFresh\NARetail\Catalog\Buyability\FailedScans
  • Integrated the solution into AFS Defects WBR through a Failed Scans Specific Slide with Waterfall attribution
Results & Impact
  • Buyability driven defect waterfall attribution completed and live as part of Defects WBR
  • Defined 5 defects accounting for ~25% of all instore failed scans
  • Each defect has a clear path to resolution actioned weekly through existing F3RS SOPs
  • Business value of $800K (labor savings across tech, non-tech & support ops + OPS loss + shrink)

Report on ASIN Readiness Metrics for LTO Selection

Situation
Previously, defect checks for LTO ASINs were focused on Procurability rate, which was misleading for manually ordered LTOs. Additional gaps surfaced post defect gate, resulting in items blocked from DC slotting or planogramming. In Q1, LTO item readiness checks expanded to include SCCR and Slotting, which required tracking of 5+ metrics with 10+ SIM resolution paths, making it difficult to reconcile item readiness and maintain accountability with action owners and leadership. Due to ordering nuances for LTO ASINs and reoccurring issues with traditional defect checks, the business needed a single ASIN readiness metric to ensure ASINs being passed off for POG build were procurable, buyable, POG ready, and slotting ready at the defect gate.
Task
  • Develop a singular readiness metric to check all blockers in one check and provide an accurate % readiness metric vs tracking 5+ different metrics for LTO assortment readiness
  • Create methodology for tracking readiness across four key areas (Procurability, Buyability, SCCR, DC Slotting)
  • Establish a calculation formula and tracking mechanism
  • Define clear requirements for each check category
  • Implement an automated reporting method for the new metric developed through this process
Action
  • Developed the metric combining 5+ defect and catalog checks into one which is currently used by the defect team and action owners to identify blockers to Assortment Readiness
  • Created tracking system at the ASIN+FC level for accurate representation of readiness
  • Established comprehensive checks across four key areas with specific attribute requirements
  • Set up daily scheduled datanet job with the reporting included in weekly events defects email as well as the Defects WBR reporting for LTO events
Results & Impact
  • This updated metric consolidates 5+ defect and catalog checks into one metric, allowing users to refer to one Assortment Readiness metric vs different defect rates across different metrics and teams
  • Since a validated sourcing input is a baseline requirement of the metric, the report also checks all blockers at the same time, as opposed to a waterfall logic checking one issue at a time. This provides users with a clear call to action during LTO event defect sprints, resulting in less firefighting and clear selection cut decisions at the defect gate
  • LTO Assortment Readiness Report is currently used to communicate progress, track completeness of required attributes, and flag necessary actions for LTO ASINs prior to a defect gate
  • Report helps Category teams achieve the goal of 100% Assortment Readiness by an LTO Event defect gate. Any ASIN that is not Event Ready will be cut from selection at the defect gate
  • Anecdotally category teams have said the new metric has helped simplify the actions needed to close out defect gaps during the defect gate
